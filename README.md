# Mobile-Sales-Dashboard

## Overview
A Mobile Sales Dashboard in Power BI provides a clear and interactive view of mobile sales performance, helping businesses track revenue, units sold, and profit margins. It enables users to analyze trends, identify top-selling models, and monitor regional sales distribution.

The dashboard integrates data from multiple sources, offering insights into customer preferences, sales channels, and inventory levels. With real-time updates, businesses can optimize stock, evaluate marketing impact, and improve decision-making.

Interactive filters and drill-downs allow for detailed analysis, making it a powerful tool for forecasting demand, enhancing sales strategies, and driving business growth

## Key Features

1. Time-Based Sales Analysis

    * Quarterly Sales Trends: Track sales performance for each quarter over the past three years.

    * Last 3-Year Results: Compare yearly sales growth, revenue trends, and seasonal patterns.

    * Month-wise Comparison: Analyze monthly fluctuations to identify peak and slow sales periods.

    * Same Period Last Year (YoY Analysis): Compare current sales data with the same period from the previous year to measure growth or decline.

2. Data Visualization & Charts

    * Line Charts: Show sales trends over months, quarters, and years.

    * Bar Charts: Compare product performance, sales channels, and revenue by region.

    * Pie Charts: Break down revenue by brand, category, or customer segment.
  
3. Dynamic Filters & Drill-Throughs

    * Month Filter: Select specific months to compare sales trends.

    * Payment Mode Filter: Analyze sales based on payment methods (Credit Card, UPI, Cash, EMI, etc.).

    * Mobile Model Filter: View sales data for different mobile brands and models.

    * Sales Channel Filters: Differentiate between online and offline sales data.
  
## How This Data Enhances Power BI Visuals

Incorporating time-based sales data, payment modes, and mobile models into Power BI enhances visuals by enabling deeper insights, better comparisons, and dynamic reporting. Here’s how:

1. Improved Trend Analysis

    * Line Charts: Show monthly, quarterly, and yearly trends, helping to identify sales patterns and seasonality.

    * YoY & QoQ Comparisons: Using "Same Period Last Year" or quarterly comparisons, businesses can track growth or decline over time.


2. Enhanced Comparison & Performance Evaluation

    * Bar & Column Charts: Compare mobile models, brands, and payment modes, making it easy to spot top-selling products.

    * Stacked Charts: Show sales distribution across different payment methods (Credit Card, UPI, Cash, etc.).
